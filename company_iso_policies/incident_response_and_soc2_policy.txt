ACME CORPORATION — SECURITY MONITORING, INCIDENT RESPONSE, AND SOC 2 CONTROL POLICY
Document owner: Security Operations
Applies to: Production systems and supporting corporate services

Security monitoring
- Authentication, privileged access, configuration changes, and security alerts are centrally logged.
- Security Operations monitors critical alerts continuously and triages high-severity alerts within 30 minutes.
- Production security logs are retained for 365 days and protected from unauthorized modification.
- Five failed authentication attempts within 10 minutes generate a high-severity alert.
- Any access by a terminated user is a critical incident requiring immediate session revocation.

Incident response
- Suspected incidents must be recorded in the incident tracking system with severity, owner, timestamps, evidence, containment, and recovery actions.
- Critical incidents must be escalated to the incident commander within 15 minutes.
- Post-incident reviews are completed within five business days and corrective actions are tracked to closure.
- Incident response simulations occur at least annually, with attendance and lessons learned retained.

SOC 2 operating evidence
- Control owners retain dated evidence that controls operated throughout the applicable review period.
- Quarterly user access reviews must show reviewer identity, scope, exceptions, resolution, and completion date.
- Change records require documented testing and approval before production deployment.
- Vendor risk reviews occur before onboarding and annually thereafter for critical vendors.
- Backups run daily; restoration tests occur quarterly and results are retained.

Data protection
- Confidential data is encrypted with approved cryptography in transit and at rest.
- Payment card data and government identifiers may not appear in application logs.
- Data retention and deletion follow the approved records schedule and legal holds.
